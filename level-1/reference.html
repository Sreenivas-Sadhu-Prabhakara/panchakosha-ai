The prompt cheat sheet

C · E · F · T — reach for these four and any prompt improves:

Context

Who you are + the situation.

Example

Show a sample of what "good" looks like.

Format

Bullets? Email? Table? How long?

Tone

Warm, formal, funny, blunt.

Plus three habits: iterate (follow up to refine), bring real material (your text, your numbers), and verify facts (ask for a source you can click).

10 copy-me daily prompts

Inbox zero

"Draft a polite reply that declines this but keeps the door open: [paste]"

Decode jargon

"Explain this contract paragraph like I'm 15: [paste]"

Meal from fridge

"Here's a photo of my fridge — 3 dinners I can make tonight?"

Trip in a box

"1-day [city] itinerary, [budget], with source links for hours/prices."

Study buddy

"Teach me [topic] in 5 steps, then quiz me."

Meeting notes

"Summarize this thread into decisions, owners, open questions: [paste]"

Gift ideas

"5 gift ideas for [person], likes [x], budget [y], not clichéd."

Health translate

"Explain these lab results in plain terms — and what to ask my doctor. (I'll still verify.)"

Fix my writing

"Tighten this to half the length, keep my voice: [paste]"

Argue both sides

"Give the strongest case for and against [decision I'm weighing]."

Plain-English glossary

Prompt
The message you send the assistant.
Model
The "brain" behind the tool (e.g. Claude Opus). Bigger/newer usually = smarter, sometimes slower.
Hallucination
When the AI states something false but confidently. Always verify facts that matter.
Context / memory
What the assistant can "see" — the current conversation, plus anything you attach.
Token
The small chunk of text a model reads/writes in. You'll meet these properly in Level 3.
